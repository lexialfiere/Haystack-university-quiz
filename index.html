🏭 Our Story

Our story begins in the 1930s, back when this area was referred to as "Hojack Country." The waterfront was bustling and alive with tourists visiting by the thousands, traveling the Great Gorge Railway. The Lewiston Silo was home to the coal that fueled these steamers, and played an important role in housing the fuel that kept the waterfront alive. Locals set up stands to sell souvenirs and novelty items to visitors.

The demise of the Great Gorge Railway in 1938, the construction of a superhighway to Toronto, and excessive pollution strangling the river caused the waterfront activity to decline. An ice jam destroyed the terminal and The Silo sat alone, bearing witness to the piling of sand dredged from the river bottom and the quiet sounds of pigeons roosting in the top of the decaying superstructure.

In 1998, The Silo was reborn as a restaurant and has become a beloved local landmark known for quick service, Haystacks, friendly staff, and clean dining.

⭐ The Haystack

The Haystack is our signature item — steak and mozzarella cheese in a hoagie roll with crunchy hash browns on top and a little mayo in the roll. It was featured on Man vs. Food and put the Silo on the map. Being precise with every Haystack — the right amount of cheese, fully scraping cans — keeps food cost in check so we can pay our team better wages.

🤝 Customer Service — The Silo Standard
  • Always greet every customer. When someone walks in: "Hi, welcome to the Silo, how can I help you?" — no exceptions.
  • Never ignore a passing customer. If they're eating, ask how everything is. A simple acknowledgment makes people feel valued.
  • Move with urgency. Walk quickly to the register when you see a customer. Short lines are a top priority — long lines lose business.
  • Upsell every order. Ask if they'd like to make it a meal with fries and a drink. It helps the business and gives customers a better experience.
  • A smile goes a long way. Make eye contact. Be warm. Customers remember how you made them feel more than anything else.
  • Handle complaints with grace. Listen fully, apologize sincerely, and get a manager. Never argue, never dismiss. One bad experience can cost us many customers.
  • Anticipate needs. Refill napkins before they run out. Notice a dirty table before a customer has to sit at it. Be one step ahead.
  • The milkshake handoff. Always explain the caboose process: "Your milkshake will be made at our ice cream caboose — go right over, your buzzer will ring and they'll call it out the window. You do not need to wait in line."
  • Family and friends visiting? Give them 10% off their order.
  • The customer's last impression matters as much as the first. A friendly goodbye and a clean table make people come back.
📋 Workplace Policies
  • Phone use: On break only. Never on the cook line or in front of customers.
  • Hair: Tight bun — no flyaways, no ponytails.
  • Breaks: Outside tables only. No smoking or vaping anywhere on property — you will be written up. No leaving the premises while on the clock.
  • NYS meal break: A 30-minute unpaid meal break is required for shifts over 6 consecutive hours.
  • Overtime: Do not pick up shifts past 40 hours without checking with a manager first.
  • No call no shows: Not acceptable. Find a cover and notify a manager. Failure to do so results in a write-up with possible termination.
  • Complaints / hours / pay: Talk to Lexi directly — never to coworkers.
  • Nothing on the ground: Everything on a shelf — including boxes.
  • Closing: 40 minutes. Everyone off the clock within an hour of closing.
  • Every sink (including bathrooms): Must have soap, paper towels, and hot water per NYS law.
  • Downtime: Find something to clean, organize, or restock. Never stand around.
  • Mental health: Don't pick up shifts on your day off. Go for a walk, do something you enjoy, drink water. We care about you outside of work too.
🔄 Stocking & Cleanliness
  • Every station: Bleach bucket with half cap bleach, warm water, and its own rag.
  • Stocking rule: Oldest to the front, newest to the back — always. Beer, custard, bread bags, everything.
  • Before leaving any shift: Stock, clean, and change garbage. Bring up low ice cream, cheese, beer, etc. Reset everything for the next shift.
  • Closing: Restock all food, all coolers, low ice cream, all paper products. Floors and counters spotless — even under equipment.
  • Late customers get the same experience. We serve customers up until a few minutes past closing. A customer at 8:50pm should get the same quality and speed as one at 1:00pm. Clean the grill and equipment quickly — never slow down service because it's close to closing.
  • Deck windows: Must be either fully up and strapped or fully down and buttoned. Never leave them halfway — the wind will make them blow up and down, ruining the windows and potentially hurting a customer.
🍳 Kitchen
  • Temps: Chicken 165°F · Ground beef 160°F · Pork 145°F · All coolers below 40°F
  • Danger zone: 40°F–140°F. Food cannot sit in this range for more than 2 hours.
  • Fridge order: Cheese on top → items organized by required internal cooking temperature → chicken alone on the very bottom.
  • Thawing: Cold running water or in the fridge early. Never leave out in the basement.
  • Gloves: Change after touching raw meat, and whenever soiled or contaminated.
  • Squirt bottles: All must be labeled — even water.
  • Arriving: Personal items away → hair net → wash hands.
  • Grill/flat top: Manual scrubbing, no chemicals. Charcoal brick on flat top. Scraping and scrubbing on grill.
  • Bread pull: Morning pull based on weather — lower on bad weather days.
  • Produce turning: Never throw it out without telling a manager. Text Lexi — we may get supplier credits.
  • Phones & vapes: Cannot be in the sight line of customers — this is a major health violation.
  • Chemicals & dirty mats: Never in view of customers or in your work zone. Our reputation matters.
🖥️ Register
  • Greeting: "Hi, welcome to the Silo, how can I help you?"
  • Two main focuses: Clean tables and short lines — then sweep deck, clean condiment stations, change garbage.
  • Upsell: Ask every customer if they want to make it a meal with fries and a drink.
  • Milkshake process: Always explain the caboose. They do not wait in line again.
  • Refunds: Always get a manager — never process without approval.
  • Tips: Counted as the very last step. The person counting must be off the clock.
  • Deck windows: Must be either fully up and strapped or fully down and buttoned — never left halfway. Wind will make them flap, which ruins the windows and can hurt a customer.
🍦 Ice Cream / Caboose
  • Scoop consistency: Same size as your coworker. Every customer gets the same experience.
  • Custard thawing: Put in fridge early whenever there are open spots.
  • Machine running low: Fill with fully thawed custard.
  • Milkshake from inside: Make immediately, call on pager, call out LOUDLY through the window.
  • Greetings: "Hi! How can I help you?" / "Sorry it's so loud, what was that?" / "Anything else today?"
  • Tips: Counted as the very last step. The person counting must be off the clock.
🌟 The Basics of Great Customer Service

Great service isn't complicated — it's consistency. Greet every person within 10 seconds of them walking in. Use their name if you know it. When someone asks a question you don't know the answer to, never say "I don't know" and leave it there — say "Let me find out for you." Customers don't remember what they ordered, but they remember how they were treated. A warm tone, eye contact, and genuine urgency tell a customer they matter. The goal isn't just to serve food — it's to give people a reason to come back.

🧹 Why a Clean Restaurant Matters

A customer decides whether they trust your food within the first 5 seconds of walking in. Dirty tables, sticky floors, or overflowing garbage tell them everything they need to know — and they won't say anything, they just won't come back. A clean restaurant also keeps your team safe: grease buildup causes slips, clutter causes accidents, and poor sanitation leads to health code violations that can shut a business down. Clean as you go, not just at close. If you see it, fix it — don't wait to be asked.

💼 Employee Etiquette — The Professional Standard

Show up on time, dressed right, ready to work. Don't bring drama from home onto the floor. Speak to coworkers the way you'd want a customer to hear you — because they can. Never bad-mouth the business, your managers, or your coworkers to anyone, on or off the clock. When you're on shift, your phone is away. If you have downtime, find work — there is always something to clean, stock, or organize. Professionalism isn't about being perfect. It's about being reliable, respectful, and taking pride in what you do.
